OVERVIEW

Searching for new genes involved in the heterogeneous group of autoinflammatory diseases with a similar phenotype CAPS (cryopyrin associated periodic syndromes)

CAPS autoinflammatory diseases are rare, autosomal dominant, associated with the activation of the inflammatory response in the apparent absence of infectious events. Approximately 70% of patients a de novo mutation in the NLRP3 gene, in a small percentage due to mosaicism not detectable by the Sanger method.

Thanks to the collaboration with clinical distributed throughout the national territory, are collecting PBMCs of patients with CAPS phenotype but no detectable mutation to the gene NLRP3 and parents willing to participate in the study. It has the sequence exome of two patients who had been excluded the presence of mosaicism at NLRP3.

BACKGROUND

CAPS are rare monogenic conditions belonging to autoinflammatory diseases (AIDS), whose common clinical feature is the activation of the inflammatory response in apparent absence of infectious events. They are due to mutations of genes encoding proteins that play a key role in the regulation of the inflammatory response, thus explaining the phenotype associated with. Although the clinical manifestations of AIDs are often overlapping, the CAPS are characterized in particular by atypical cutaneous chronic or recurrent urticaria, associated In addition to various systemic symptoms. Therefore, unlike other AIDS, CAPS generally have a specific phenotype, though this does not correspond to omogeneia 'of genetic defects. About 70% of CAPS patients have a mutation in the NLRP3 gene, a small percentage of which are in a state of mosaic. In addition, to date, mutations in the gene NLRP12 have been identified in only six different patients are characterized by a specific phenotype: sensitivity 'in the cold. The remaining affected individuals have a genetic diagnosis and are therefore referred to as CAPS-like. The identification of other genes is desirable not only to provide a correct genetic diagnosis to patients and to assess the risk of recurrence, but also to identify new molecular targets for alternative therapeutic approaches to the antagonist currently used, including one for the IL1beta (anakinra) which are expensive, need to subcutaneous injections weekly and recently showed resistance phenomena.

Experimental design

Patients with a clinical and immunological phenotype typical of CAPS are regularly submitted to our laboratory for ten years screening for the mutation of exon 3 of NLRP3 and, in the case of sensitivity 'to cold, exon 3 of NLRP12. To pursue the goal of identifying new genes responsible for the development of CAPS, we selected patients results negative for the screening of the total coding sequence of the two genes NLRP, even for the condition of mosaic in the case of the gene NLRP3 (analyzed by means of next generation sequencing coverage 1000X). DNA from patients without mutation will undergo whole exome sequencing (WES) in order to identify mutations in new genes. The analysis WES will 'conduct also on the DNA of those parents who agree to participate in the study. In this case, the statistical analysis will be 'based on the search of a de novo mutation, thus increasing the power of the study. The promising candidate genes will be validated with the Sanger method. Functional studies will be suitably set for each new gene, on the basis of its specific role phone, and conducted in the cells of patients and / or in cell lines.

Sequencing

Sequence Alignment Methodology

The alignments were processed using the procedure described be 1000g consortium (Gil A McVean et al., Nature 2012) including the variant call. We aligned the reads got from the Illumina run, against the reference “Human genome” (release: hg19), using the BWA software (Li H. and Durbin R., 2009), version 0.7.5-r404.

Variants calling

Variants calling methodology

On the aligned data, we ran a standard GATK pipeline (McKenna et al, 2010) for the variant calling with base quality score recalibration, indel realignment, duplicate removal, and we performed SNP and INDEL discovery and genotyping across all samples simulatenously (adding our internal controls, see also: “Additional datasets”) using standard hard filtering parameters or variant quality score recalibration according to GATK Best Practices recommendations (DePristo et al., 2011; Van der Auwera et al., 2013). The output of Variants calling is a file in VCF Format (Petr Danecek et al., 2011).

Variant Annotation and Selection Methodology

For the variant annotation and filtering we have applied a standard SNPEff/SNPSift annotation pipeline (Cingolani et al., 2012).

From the Variant Calling pipeline we got 511999 variants (Tier0.vcf), these variants are filtered according with the standard procedure producing the following VCF files:

  1. Tier0.vcf : Annotated variants with predicted effect (N=511999).
  2. Tier1.vcf : Variants that pass the quality filters (N=276989).
  3. Tier2.vcf : Variants with an high or moderate impact in the protein (N=64368).
  4. Tier2.xlsx : Tier2.vcf file in Excel format.

Variant Annotations: Glossary

  • Know disease genes : genes that are known to be associated to the pathology
  • Private variants : variants that have no record in dbSNP and are present only in one family (or on sample for sporadic case)
  • New variants : variants that have no record in dbSNP
  • Rare variants : variants with a MAF <= 0.02 and allele count <= 8
